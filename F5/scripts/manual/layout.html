Getting Started

Wireframing

Starting an application with wireframes might look time consuming but actually knowing from the beginning where you are heading will save a lot of time. Wireframes can be done with pen and paper or using wireframe softwares (sketch ui , adobe experience XD).

ThA wireframe is the blueprint of websites / applications and mobile apps. The main purpose is to understand what you are building and show the client how the app will be structure before designing.

Not using wireframes and jumping directly to developing an app might cause distress. Clients may like it but they might want to move a menu from the left on the right and then he wants the menu larger , smaller and so on creating inconsistency and wasting precious time rather than planning ahead.

Elements of a wireframe

Wireframes should contain depending on the needs of the app a header , footer , sidebar and content areas and elements such as buttons , widgets etc.

    Structure
  • Content - what will be displayed in the page
  • Structure - how are the elements connected
  • Hierarchy - how elements will be positioned , labeled and sized
  • Functionality - how will they work together
  • Behaviour - how does the elements interact
